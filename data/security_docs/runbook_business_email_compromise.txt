Title: Business Email Compromise Response
Severity: Critical
MITRE ATT&CK: T1566.002, T1534
Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 15-Nov-2025
Author: Security Operations Team
Environment: Email, Office 365, Financial Systems
Tools: Email Security Gateway, Office 365 Security Center, SIEM, Email Forensics
DETECTION:

Executive/CFO email account compromise indicators
Unusual wire transfer or payment requests
Email forwarding rules to external addresses
Invoice fraud reports from vendors
Spoofed executive emails requesting transfers
Email account login from unusual locations
Mailbox delegation changes

INVESTIGATION:

Check compromised email account:

Login history (IPs, locations, devices)
Email forwarding rules and delegates
Sent items for fraudulent requests
Deleted items recovery
Inbox rules and filters


Review financial transaction requests:

Requested transfer amounts and destinations
Recipient bank account details
Timeline of fraudulent requests
Who received and acted on requests?


Analyze email headers:

Spoofed sender addresses vs legitimate
SPF/DKIM/DMARC authentication results
Source IP addresses


Interview affected employees:

Who received suspicious requests?
Were any transfers completed?
Normal approval processes followed?



IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:

Suspend compromised email account immediately
Contact financial institution to halt wire transfers
Alert accounting/finance team to verify all recent requests
Remove malicious email forwarding rules
Reset account password and revoke sessions

CONTAINMENT:

Review all email accounts for similar compromise indicators
Check for additional compromised accounts
Remove unauthorized mailbox delegates and permissions
Verify no additional fraudulent emails sent
Review financial transactions for unauthorized activity
Enable MFA for all email accounts if not active
Implement email authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

POST-INCIDENT:

Document financial losses and recovery efforts
Report to FBI IC3 (Internet Crime Complaint Center)
Notify cyber insurance carrier
Employee training on BEC tactics
Review and strengthen payment authorization procedures
Legal consultation for fund recovery
Notify affected vendors/partners if their information used

LESSONS LEARNED:

Mandatory MFA for all email accounts
Email banner warnings for external emails
Out-of-band verification for financial requests
Regular security awareness training on BEC
Implement email authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
Dual approval process for wire transfers
Email security gateway with advanced threat protection
Monitor for unusual email forwarding rules
